💡 Strategy & Leadership: The Blueprint

McKinsey Standard: Implementing VRIO, Blue Ocean (ERRC), and formal goal hierarchies.

2.1 Goal and Metric Hierarchy (The Foundation)

CSFs, OKRs, & KPIs

CSFs: Critical Success Factors (3-5 non-negotiable mission areas).

OKRs: Objectives & Key Results (Ambitious, measurable goals set quarterly).

KPIs: Key Performance Indicators (Metrics to track ongoing performance).

Foundational Strategy

VRIO Analysis: A competitive advantage must be Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, and Organized.

Blue Ocean: Create new markets using the **ERRC** framework (Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create).

2.2 Multi-Generational Leadership

Tailor your approach based on core values and preferred communication.

  • Gen X: Values Autonomy and **Balance**. Lead by focusing on **outcomes**.
  • Gen Z: Values Individuality and **Mental Health**. Prioritize **flexible schedules** and clear expectations.

🗣️ Comms & Influence: The Output

Rigor: Using structured conflict resolution and narrative models for clarity.

3.1 High-Stakes Conversation Frameworks

The PREP Method

Point, Reason, Example, Point. Use for concise conviction. (Start with the answer upfront).

The A.R.E. Method

Answer, Reason, Evidence. Use when challenged; back your claims with data/facts.

Strategic Storytelling

Use the SPARK (Set the Scene, Present Problem...) framework for difficult news. **Keep Your Message Simple** (Steve Jobs' Rule).

3.2 High-Performer Communication Skills

Focus on **Active Listening**, **Clarity and Conciseness**, and **Empathy**. For conflict, aim for **win-win outcomes** and provide **Constructive Feedback** (focus on growth, not criticism).

⏳ Productivity & Execution: The Engine

McKinsey Standard: Efficiency optimization via 80/20 analysis and structured project flow.

4.1 Time Management & Focus

Prioritization Models

• Eisenhower Matrix: Urgent vs. Important (Do, Decide, Delegate, Delete).

• Eat That Frog: Do the most important/difficult task first (Anti-procrastination).

• Pickle Jar Theory: Schedule the "Big Rocks" (Important tasks) first.

The 80/20 Rules

Productivity: Focus on the 20% of tasks that drive 80% of results.

Decision Mastery: Analyze the key data that influences 80% of major decisions.

4.2 Delegation and Team Management

Delegate the 80% of low-impact tasks. Use the **70% Rule** (If someone can do it 70% as well, delegate it).

The D.E.L.E.G.A.T.E. Process

Define → Establish Expectations → List Resources → Empower → Guide → Assess → Thank → Evaluate.

🤖 AI & Tech Fluency: The Future

MIT Standard: Technical architectures, code logic, and precise prompt mechanics.

5.1 Prompt Engineering Mastery

Use structured formats for precise results:

  • TREF: Task, Requirement, Expectation, Format.
  • SOAR: Situation, Objective, Action, Result.
  • Parameters: Control creativity by adjusting Temperature (e.g., 0.2 for factual) and avoiding repetition with **Frequency Penalty**.

5.2 Data and Architecture Fundamentals

SQL Joins (Data Retrieval)

INNER JOIN: Matching records in both tables.

LEFT JOIN: All records from left table (A) plus matches from right (B).

FULL JOIN: All records when there's a match in either A or B.

AI in SDLC

Generative AI accelerates: Requirement Definition (analyzing feedback), Code Development (drafting), and Review/Test (automatic testing).

🚀 Career & Finance: The Win

Rigor: Applying negotiation frameworks and financial ratio analysis.

6.1 Interviewing and Salary Negotiation

Interview Frameworks

STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

CAR: Challenge, Action, Result.

PAR: Problem, Action, Result (Value added).

Negotiation Checklist

1. Research salaries BEFORE the interview.

2. DO NOT disclose current salary (limits leverage).

3. Salary is only ONE PART (value benefits, growth).

6.2 Core Financial Literacy

Know the difference:

  • EBITDA (Operating Profit) vs. **Net Income** (Final Profit after taxes/interest).
  • Liquidity: Ability to meet short-term debt (Measured by **Current Ratio**).
  • CAPEX: Long-term investment in assets (for future revenue).
